Civility Compass is a practical, SHRM-aligned diagnostic tool that helps
organiz
Submitted by: Kristi Meacham | Team:
Problem & Solution
Civility Compass is a practical, SHRM-aligned diagnostic tool that helps organizations
identify where workplace friction may be rooted in incivility, weak psychological safety,
manager inaction, or broader culture breakdowns — and what to do next.
The need is real. SHRM’s Civility Index shows that workplace incivility remains common,
with recent workplace scores staying in Zone 3, the “Take Action” range. SHRM’s research
also shows that incivility carries a significant business cost through lost productivity,
absenteeism, lower morale, and higher turnover risk. Managers are a major variable: in
recent waves, many workers said their manager could have done more to prevent incivility
and cared more about business objectives than how people are treated.
Civility Compass turns that research into action.
A user enters a workplace issue in plain language — for example, tension after layoffs,
repeated interruptions in meetings, passive-aggressive communication, exclusion, or
conflict around political conversations. The tool analyzes the situation through a SHRM
civility lens and returns a structured diagnosis, such as civility breakdown, manager-
enabled incivility, low psychological safety, communication breakdown, or change-related
civility stress. It then identifies key signals, assesses qualitative risk, surfaces likely root-
cause hypotheses, and generates practical next steps.
What makes this tool different is that it does not jump straight to blame or legal conclusions.
It is designed to help HR, managers, and leaders frame problems more clearly and respond
more constructively. It focuses on patterns, not accusations. It helps users separate
symptoms from causes, distinguish isolated tension from broader culture issues, and
identify when a problem may warrant direct HR or ER review.
The recommendations are grounded in SHRM’s broader workplace culture framework:
honest and unbiased management, civil behavior, meaningful work and opportunities, open
communication, and empathy. They are also informed by SHRM’s findings that civility and
psychological safety lead to better difficult conversations, stronger trust, greater innovation,
and longer expected employee tenure.
In short, Civility Compass helps organizations move from vague tension and reactive

Why HR Professionals Need a Better Multistate Law Comparison Tool:
For HR professionals operating across multiple states, compliance is not just complex, it’s
constantly evolving. Each state, and often local jurisdictions, enforces its own requirements
governing wages, leave, discrimination, benefits, and workplace safety.

and increases the risk of noncompliance. HR teams must balance the need for consistency in
policies with the reality of jurisdiction-specific obligations—often under tight time
constraints.

information, they often present challenges that limit usability and effectiveness:
-Fragmented Content: Legal requirements are buried in dense text or inconsistent formats,
making cross-state comparisons difficult.
-Limited Visibility: The absence of visual structure makes it hard to quickly identify key
differences and similarities.
-Lack of Actionability: Most tools, including SHRM's, stop at explaining the law, without
helping HR professionals translate requirements into practical policies or next steps.
Our Pay Transparency Comparison Tool is an enhanced, chart-based comparison tool that
transforms how HR professionals engage with multistate compliance:
-Side-by-Side Visual Comparisons: Intuitive charts allow users to instantly compare legal
requirements across jurisdictions, reducing cognitive load and saving time.
-Actionable Guidance: Built-in recommendations, best practices, and compliance checklists
help translate legal requirements into clear, implementable policies.
-Expert-Driven Insights: Up-to-date legal analysis ensures accuracy and reliability,
minimizing the risk of misinterpretation or oversight.
-Proactive Risk Identification: Highlighted differences and potential pitfalls enable HR
teams to anticipate and mitigate compliance risks before they escalate.
This enhanced solution streamlines multistate compliance, reduces errors, and empowers
HR teams to act with greater speed and confidence.
